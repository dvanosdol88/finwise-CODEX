fiduciary

/fəˈdjuːʃiˌɛri/

noun A person or firm that is legally and ethically required to act in the client’s best interest, putting the client’s interests ahead of their own and avoiding (or clearly disclosing and managing) conflicts of interest.

In plain English: a fiduciary is accountable to you, not to commissions, product quotas, or sales incentives.
